Most famous signers:

Thomas Jefferson
He was the primary author of the Declaration and is perhaps the most famous of the signers. He went on to become the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A key leader in the American Revolution, Adams was a strong advocate for independence and later served as the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath and diplomat, Franklin was one of the most respected figures of the time. He helped secure crucial French support during the Revolution.
Samuel Adams
A passionate advocate for independence and a leader of the colonial resistance, Adams played a significant role in organizing the events leading to the Revolution, like the Boston Tea Party.
John Hancock
Known for his large, bold signature, Hancock was the President of the Continental Congress at the time of the signing. His name became synonymous with the act of signing documents.
Roger Sherman
One of only a few people to sign all three major founding documents (the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution).
